James Brown called me The African Soul Man. He gave me that name on the stage of Pop Across America on TNN, and I have carried it as both a gift and a responsibility ever since.

"The rubber that won World War II came from Liberia. My family paid for this world with blood and iron and sacrifice. And then I came to live in it — and was told I don't belong."

I was born in Liberia. I survived a civil war. I was lost at sea on a refugee ship. I slept under a staircase when I first arrived in America. I was promoted to manager in three months at my first job — then fired for being late once after performing on national television.

I am a Gold and Silver Medalist at the World Championships for Performing Arts. I hold the record for the longest sustained note on the United States National Anthem played on trumpet. I have been recognized as a World Peace and Fitness Ambassador in Los Angeles.

This website is dedicated to the memory of Marie Kennedy Gobah and Zina — whose love lives in everything I do.
